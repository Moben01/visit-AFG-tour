About Kandahar Tourism

Kandahar is a city of profound historical significance and cultural richness, known as the traditional heartland of the Pashtun people. From the ancient bazaars to the surrounding orchards, Kandahar offers visitors an authentic Afghan experience.

Architectural Splendour: Mosques, Mausoleums & Historic Sites

Kandahar is home to several important Islamic architectural sites. The Shrine of the Cloak houses a relic believed to be the cloak of Prophet Muhammad. The Friday Mosque (Masjid Jame) showcases traditional Islamic architecture, while the Mausoleum of Ahmad Shah Durrani honors Afghanistan's founding father. The old city features traditional mud-brick homes and narrow alleyways that have stood for centuries.

A Rich Pashtun Cultural Heritage

Kandahar is the cultural center of Pashtun traditions. The city celebrates Nowruz with family gatherings and visits to local shrines. Traditional Pashtun hospitality is legendary, with guests treated to elaborate meals featuring Kandahari naan, roast lamb, and fresh pomegranates. The city is also known for its distinctive embroidery and handicrafts.

Explore Arghandab Valley and Surrounding Orchards

The lush Arghandab Valley surrounding Kandahar is famous for its pomegranate and grape orchards. Visitors can enjoy walks through the fruit gardens, especially beautiful during harvest season. The valley also offers scenic views of the Hindu Kush mountains and opportunities for picnics along the Arghandab River.

Shop for Traditional Handicrafts and Fresh Fruits

Kandahar's bazaars are famous for high-quality pomegranates, grapes, and melons. The city is also known for its traditional handicrafts including intricate embroidery (khamak), leather goods, and copperware. The Shah Bazaar and Herat Bazaar are excellent places to find authentic Kandahari products.

From Kandahari Naan to Roast Lamb: Enjoy Local Cuisine

Kandahar's cuisine is renowned throughout Afghanistan. Kandahari naan (large, oblong bread) is a specialty, as is roast lamb (shinwari) and various rice dishes. The city is particularly famous for its pomegranates which are used in both sweet and savory dishes. Meals are often accompanied by green tea and fresh yogurt.

Traditional Hospitality in Local Guesthouses

While Kandahar doesn't have luxury resorts, visitors can experience authentic Pashtun hospitality in local guesthouses. These accommodations offer simple but comfortable rooms, home-cooked meals, and the chance to experience traditional Kandahari lifestyle firsthand.

Explore the Historic Sites of Kandahar

Kandahar offers visitors the chance to explore important historical sites including the Shrine of the Cloak, the Mausoleum of Ahmad Shah Durrani, and the ancient Chilzina rock chambers. The city's old quarter features traditional architecture and bustling bazaars that have changed little over the centuries.

Spiritual Sites and Historic Mosques

Kandahar is home to several important Islamic sites that attract pilgrims from across Afghanistan. The Friday Mosque is particularly beautiful, while the Shrine of the Cloak is one of Afghanistan's most sacred sites. Religious festivals are celebrated with great devotion in Kandahar.

Top 10 Places to Visit in Kandahar

Shrine of the Cloak — Houses a relic believed to be the cloak of Prophet Muhammad.

Mausoleum of Ahmad Shah Durrani — Tomb of Afghanistan's founding father.

Chilzina — Ancient rock chambers with inscriptions from the Mughal era.

Arghandab Valley — Lush orchards and scenic landscapes.

Friday Mosque (Masjid Jame) — Kandahar's most important mosque.

Shah Bazaar — Traditional market selling fruits, spices and handicrafts.

Herat Bazaar — Famous for Kandahari embroidery and textiles.

Baba Wali Shrine — Popular pilgrimage site in Arghandab Valley.

Kandahar Museum — Showcases artifacts from the region's rich history.

Pomegranate Orchards — Visit during harvest season (September-October).

Best Time for Visiting Kandahar

The best time to visit Kandahar is from October to April when temperatures are milder. Spring (March-April) is particularly beautiful when the orchards are in bloom. Summers can be extremely hot, with temperatures often exceeding 40°C (104°F).

How to Reach Kandahar?

By Air

Kandahar International Airport connects the city with domestic flights to Kabul and some international destinations in the region.

By Road

Kandahar is connected to other major cities via the Ring Road. The journey from Kabul takes about 8-10 hours depending on security conditions.

By Train

Kandahar currently has no train services; air and road travel are the main options for reaching the city.
