What do Afghanis look like?

Afghan currency is called the Afghani (AFN). Coins are rarely used in daily transactions, but when available, they include denominations such as 1, 2, and 5 Afghanis, typically made of metal with national symbols and inscriptions in Dari or Pashto.

More commonly, the Afghani is used in the form of colourful banknotes, ranging from AFN10 to AFN1,000 (approximately USD0.13 to USD13.50). Each note features cultural and historical landmarks of Afghanistan, such as the Blue Mosque (Shrine of Hazrat Ali) in Mazar-i-Sharif, Darul Aman Palace in Kabul, and images of Afghan wildlife, traditional patterns, and national emblems.

The designs reflect the rich heritage and diverse identity of Afghanistan.
