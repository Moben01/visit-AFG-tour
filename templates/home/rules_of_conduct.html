There have been political and social changes in the country that require us to respect the norms and values, taking into account all the nuances, we will make the tour useful for our guests, where they will feel most welcome.

Below we present to our guests the recommendations from the Ministry of Information and Culture of Afghanistan for foreign tourists. This will help you prepare and have a wonderful tour of the country:

You are responsible for travel insurance, which should cover any activity you plan to undertake, from driving to mountaineering.

Make sure you have a photocopy of your passport in case you are asked to present it on arrival or at any time during your travel. In the meantime, be sure to keep and protect your passport while travelling in the country.

Keep in touch with family and friends in your home country. We suggest sharing a copy of your tour itinerary with return flight schedules, city names and accommodation arrangements.

Dress like the locals. We recommend carrying loose, long-sleeved dresses and, for women, a scarf to cover the head and shoulders. Sleeveless T-shirts and dresses are prohibited for everyone throughout the country and especially in village and city markets.

Please do not carry excess cash or expensive jewellery. If you want to buy souvenirs in Afghanistan, buy them from official shops that sell legally. In addition, buying or taking away historical antiquities without a receipt and from an unauthorized dealer is considered illegal; so always ask your guide for advice.

Alcohol is prohibited in the country, so do not bring any alcohol or anything that may get you into trouble upon arrival. Meanwhile, drug use is prohibited in the country.

Please carry cash and a money belt. Debit/credit cards (Visa, Mastercard, etc.) are not accepted in Afghanistan. You can convert currency to Afghani in your home country or at Kabul airport. You can also ask your guide to help you with the currency conversion upon arrival.

Always travel with a local guide. Taking photographs of government buildings, military personnel, and military installations is prohibited.

Female travellers/tourists should avoid shaking hands with local men and vice versa. This is a cultural and religious practice rather than a health and safety concern.

Avoid travelling late at night or at midnight and always ask your driver/guide for directions. We advise you to always follow the route given by your driver/guide for safety reasons.

Carry hand sanitizer, sunscreen, water and snacks when travelling in the country. Travel by road can be long but the scenery is beautiful.

Homosexuality is discouraged in Afghanistan; therefore, we advise you to avoid such public remarks as they may cause problems.

Pack the night before and get plenty of rest.
